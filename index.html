SalesOS — Plain-English B2B Lead Discovery

SalesOS helps B2B teams describe their ideal customer in plain English, review qualified prospects, enrich contact data, and launch personalized outreach faster from one workflow.

How It Works

  1. Describe your ideal customer — Enter the job titles, industries, company size, geography, or buying signals you care about in natural language.
  2. Review matched prospects — See ranked leads with useful company and contact context so you can prioritize faster.
  3. Launch outreach — Generate more relevant outbound messages and manage replies without bouncing between disconnected tools.

Key Features

Plain-English lead search
Find B2B prospects without relying on complicated filters or boolean syntax.
Lead prioritization
Review best-fit prospects first with contextual information that helps your team focus on the right accounts.
Personalized outreach
Generate outreach drafts using lead and company context so your first touch is more relevant.
Reply management
Keep conversations and follow-ups organized in one place after prospects respond.
Workflow automation
Reduce manual sales work with sequences, handoffs, and automation building blocks.

Who It's For

  • Founder-led B2B sales teams
  • Outbound agencies
  • SDR and growth teams that need leads fast
  • Teams tired of manual prospect research and tool sprawl

SalesOS offers a free starting point so you can explore the workflow before upgrading to higher-volume prospecting and outreach features.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is SalesOS?
SalesOS is a B2B prospecting and outreach platform that helps teams go from ICP definition to live outreach faster.
Do I need boolean search skills to use it?
No. SalesOS is built around plain-English targeting.
Can I try it before paying?
Yes. You can start free and upgrade when you are ready.
Who is SalesOS best for?
It is strongest for founder-led sales teams, outbound agencies, SDR teams, and B2B operators running outbound motions.
What integrations are available?
SalesOS currently supports Google Workspace and is expanding support for tools like Slack, HubSpot, Salesforce, Calendly, and Zapier.
Is SalesOS a fit for every business?
No. It is generally not the right fit for e-commerce, B2C, or companies that do not run outbound.
