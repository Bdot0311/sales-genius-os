# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Outbound-focused B2B operators who are actively selling: founder-led sales teams running their own prospecting, SDR managers at 10–200 person companies, outbound agencies prospecting on behalf of clients. They move fast, have zero patience for overhead, and have been burned by tools that overpromise. They know what good outreach looks like — they just need the pipeline to feed it without stitching Apollo + Clay + Gmail + a spreadsheet together every morning.

## Product Purpose

SalesOS removes the dead time between "I know who to target" and "I have a qualified, verified contact with a draft email ready to send." The workflow that matters — ICP → qualified prospects → verified email → personalized first-touch → sent — has always lived across 4+ tools. SalesOS is that workflow, unified. Success looks like an outbound rep going from zero to first-touch sent in under 2 minutes with no tab-switching.

## Brand Personality

Sharp · Confident · Inevitable. The tool that serious outbound operators reach for when they stop tolerating overhead. Not arrogant — earned. Sounds like a founder who's been in the trenches and built the thing they couldn't find. The voice is direct, unhedged, a little dry.

## Anti-references

- **Apollo / ZoomInfo** — enterprise data-tool coldness: cluttered UI, feature-grid marketing, zero warmth
- **Generic AI SaaS** — purple gradients on white, stock robot imagery, "supercharge your workflow" filler copy, looks like every other tool launched in 2024
- **Webflow templates** — over-animated, gradient-everything, no visual hierarchy, everything tries to be the hero
- **HubSpot / Salesforce** — corporate orange/blue, committee-designed, zero personality, zero founder voice
- **AI slop broadly** — anything that looks generated, interchangeable, or built without a point of view

## Design Principles

1. **Earn every element** — if it can be removed without losing meaning, remove it. Decorative complexity is a confession that the core isn't strong enough.
2. **Confidence without volume** — make bold statements quietly. Large type. Generous space. No visual shouting.
3. **Show the mechanism** — don't describe features in the abstract; reveal the workflow in motion. The product is the proof.
4. **Founder voice, not brand voice** — every line of copy and every layout decision should feel like a person who built this, not a marketing committee that approved it.
5. **Precision over polish** — tight spacing relationships, deliberate type size ratios, considered motion timing. These matter more than glows and gradients.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. `prefers-reduced-motion` respected on all animations. Text contrast ratios ≥ 4.5:1 on dark backgrounds. Focus states visible at all times.
